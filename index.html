Literature Survey

Banana is one of the main food crops in Sri Lanka which has a huge impact on country's economy. But banana cultivation is threatened by a number of facts and among them banana diseases cause major crop losses and financial losses towards the planters [1],[2]. Among all the existing banana diseases, allegedly, 42% of reported cases caused by 'Yellow Sigatoka' disease when another 30% of reported cases are caused by the 'Panama' disease [1], making these two diseases, two major existing banana diseases in Sri Lanka. Further, these two diseases are well-known as fast spread diseases through wind, water and soil. Further, the lack of communication between farmers upon diagnosis have made a great issue in spreading these diseases.

So far, farmers have been widely using the visual observation approach to identify banana diseases which is error-prone due to the lack of knowledge among the farmers. Further, not having proper communication media between farmers have led these banana diseases to spread across several plantations under a short time period.

Further, studies about the income and the literacy level of the Sri Lankan farmers have proven that in banana disease identification, total dependency on visual inputs such as images could be problematic due to the lack of access to smartphones with good camera quality. Hence, it could be beneficial to have an approach which could use textual inputs such as symptoms as texting is a basic functionality. And also, according to surveys, it was evident that Sri Lankan farmers use both Sinhala and English Languages while texting.

Hence, in order to identify banana diseases effectively and maintain proper communication among farmers, a mobile app called 'Doctor Banana' is implemented which supports both Sinhala and English languages. This app mainly focus on the compatibility of the functions catering the needs of Sri Lankan farmers.

  • Compatible with both Panama and Yellow Sigatoka diseases.
  • Identify diseases using both visual inputs such as images as well as textual inputs such as text symptoms.
  • Provide farmers and agriculture officers with information about risk levels and risk areas of dispersion on Google Maps.
  • Notify agriculture officers about nearby banana infestations.

Research Problem

A large portion of the cultivation is frequently harmed by Panama and Sigatoka diseases because cultivators frequently misidentify and misdiagnose them or disregard the symptoms. Not having proper knowledge or following an ineffective, inaccurate prevention methods, may increase the chances of further infestations. Hence, the relevant authorities should thoroughly share information about these diseases and effective prevention methods.

In addition, approaches that exist world-wide, depends on acquiring images with higher quality which requires either a higher quality mobile phone or a camera. This can be challenging due to the limited income of farmers and limited resources farmers have.

Methodology

The proposed system is an android mobile application that uses deep learning, computer vision, and automated machine learning to diagnose illnesses with high accuracy. First, the farmer needs to select between text or images to proceed with the diagnosis.

If the farmer choose images,
Farmer has to take a picture of the infected banana leaf and uploading it to the system. The system will first verify if the provided photographs include banana leaves. After successful verification, system will proceed with the diagnosis and express result as a percentage. If the confidence level is higher, the system will recommend a treatment option for a specific leaf disease.

If the farmer choose text,
Farmer can type the symptoms of the infected banana plant using either Sinhala or English language. Then the system will analyze the inserted textual symptoms and display the results and relevant controlling measures.

If the system has identified a banana disease, the system will display the location in a map. Further, the system will acquire weather data to determine the risk level and the risk area and continue to surveillance on the detected disease.

Meanwhile, the nearest agriculture officer will be notified about the detected disease.
